C9 - Leadership is all about Compassion

Compassion and Conversation

What is your experience of compassionate (or not) leadership?

I feel that compassionate leadership is the only way to do things. It's the most basic requirement for leading others. Lacking compassion is one of the first steps to alienating your peers. It's only when leaders account for their teams' viewpoints that everyone can work effectively together.

Do the multiplying goodness meditation. How did you find it?

Invigorating and uplifting. Would do it again. :)

Do the Tonglen meditation. How did you find it?

I think I've been doing an informal version of it recently.

How does the SCARF model apply to your Phase 0 experience so far? How will it apply to bootcamp?

It's been a great experience so far. Nikau has been such a fantastic cohort. Everybody has been very supportive and down-to-earth. I'm not expecting it will be any different from that, moving forward. Except that hopefully, we'll all start feeling more competent with the tech we're learning as we move forward.

Have a difficult conversation. How did it go? (It's a valuable skill, but can take practice to develop!)

It was nearly debilitating. I have such a long road ahead and in the meantime, I need to do my best to rebalance my failures and accomplishments.
